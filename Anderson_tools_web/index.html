Tools and Softwares

Raw data

Bioinformatics FASTQ files store high-throughput sequencing data, containing nucleotide sequences and corresponding quality scores, crucial for downstream analysis in genomics research. These files enable researchers to assess sequence quality, detect variations, and explore genomic information with precision and accuracy.

Primary Tools

Bioinformatics primary QC tools assess raw data quality, identifying errors and inconsistencies, crucial for ensuring data integrity and reliable downstream analysis in genomics, proteomics, and other omics studies. These tools often include FastQC, Trimmomatic, and Fastp, offering functionalities such as read quality assessment, adapter trimming, and filtering.

Click Here

Secondary Tools

Bioinformatics secondary GATK (Genome Analysis Toolkit) tools focus on variant discovery and genotyping from high-throughput sequencing data, providing advanced algorithms for accurate variant calling and filtering, essential for identifying genetic variations in large-scale genomic studies like population genetics and clinical genomics. GATK tools like HaplotypeCaller and VariantRecalibrator are widely used for their robustness in detecting variants with high sensitivity and specificity.

Phenotypes Panel

Phenotypes Panel is typically used in genomic studies to identify and annotate various phenotypic traits associated with specific genes or genetic variations. This panel helps researchers and clinicians understand how genetic variations influence physical characteristics, disease predispositions, and other biological traits.
